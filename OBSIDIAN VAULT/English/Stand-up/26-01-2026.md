1. Verbs following "Spend time"

2. "Without" vs. "Withouts"

    Original: "...withouts error."

    Correction: "...without errors" or "error-free."

    The Rule: "Without" is a preposition and never takes an "s." To make it sound professional, use the plural "errors" or the compound adjective "error-free."

3. Future Continuous vs. Simple Future

    Original: "...i will testing..."

    Correction: "I will be testing" or "I will test."

    The Rule: You cannot follow "will" directly with an "-ing" verb. You need the helper verb "be."

        Pro-tip: In stand-ups, using "I will focus on..." or "I am prioritizing..." sounds more proactive than just "I will."
